Elephant 

                
                Elephants are the largest living land animals. Distinctive features of elephants include a long proboscis called a trunk, 
                tusks, large ear flaps, pillar-like legs, and tough but sensitive grey skin. Tusks, which are derived from the incisor teeth, 
                serve both as weapons and as tools for moving objects and digging. The large ear flaps assist in maintaining a constant body 
                temperature as well as in communication.
