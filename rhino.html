Black Rhino 

            
            Black rhino, a rhinoceros of sub-Saharan Africa having a retractable upper lip protruding in the middle. Although the species is referred 
            to as black, its colours vary from brown to grey. They are generally thought to be solitary, with the only strong bond between 
            a mother and her calf. Black rhinos are herbivorous browsers that eat leafy plants, twigs, branches, shoots, thorny wood bushes, small trees, 
            legumes, fruit, and grass.
            
            



                
                Today, there are various threats posed to black rhinos including habitat changes, illegal poaching, and competing species.
